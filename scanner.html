Smart Cart Heuristic Evaluation

As an introduction to the project, we conducted a heuristic evaluation on the new Wegman's smart carts (essentially a self-checkout in your cart that constantly updates while you shop) based on Jakob Nielsen's 10 usability heuristics:

  1. Visibility of System Status
  2. Match Between the System and the Real World
  3. User Control and Freedom
  4. Consistency and Standards
  5. Error Prevention
  6. Recognition Rather than Recall
  7. Flexibility and Efficiency of Use
  8. Aesthetic and Minimalist Design
  9. Help Users Recognize, Diagnose, and Recover from Errors
  10. Help and Documentation

For a more the full evaluation, you can take a look at the product deck—but overall, the cart didn't perform well. It was more effort than it was worth, easy to misuse (and abuse), and .

With these evaluations in mind, we set out to make our own products in effort to improve the shopping experience.

Workflow

This workflow consists of four main flows. Flows and sub-flows labels are consistent throughout the project (as well as in the project deck).

(0) Onboarding and (1) Nutrition Profile are setup process, where the user logs in or signs up and creates their personalized profile.

(A) Scan/Compare and (B) Account are daily use flows, which do include access to go back and change any preferences or settings that were declared in the previous two setup flows.
